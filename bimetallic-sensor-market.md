<p><p>A bimetallic sensor is a temperature-sensing device that utilizes two different metals with distinct thermal expansion properties. When subjected to temperature changes, the differential expansion between the metals causes bending or movement, which can be translated into a temperature reading. Commonly used in HVAC systems, automotive applications, and industrial processes, bimetallic sensors are valued for their durability and reliability in harsh environments.</p><p>The Bimetallic Sensor Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.10% during the forecast period. <p><p>The bimetallic sensor market is primarily divided into analog and digital types. Analog bimetallic sensors offer continuous readings and are typically used in applications requiring simple temperature monitoring, providing real-time data through direct measurement. In contrast, digital bimetallic sensors convert temperature readings into digital signals, enabling more precise tracking and integration with modern electronic systems. Digital sensors often feature advanced functionalities, such as data logging and remote monitoring, making them suitable for sophisticated industrial and home automation applications.</p></p>
