<p><p>The macromolecule hydrogel market is divided into two main types: natural and synthetic. Natural macromolecule hydrogels are derived from biopolymers, such as alginate, gelatin, and chitosan, offering biocompatibility and biodegradability, making them ideal for applications in medicine and agriculture. Synthetic macromolecule hydrogels, on the other hand, are engineered from artificial polymers like polyethylene glycol and polyacrylamide, allowing for greater customization of their physical and chemical properties, thus being widely used in drug delivery, tissue engineering, and absorbent products.</p></p>
